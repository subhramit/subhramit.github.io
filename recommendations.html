I remember reading about the theme of 500 Days of Summer when I was going through one of my worst breakups. Discovering it, it felt like the kind of a movie that I should have avoided at that state, and should've watched sometime in future, and ideally with someone. Then one fine midnight (when I had not yet recovered), I just said to myself - "Fuck it. I'll watch it by myself. And I'll watch it right now." Little did I know, I was about to take one of the best cinematic journeys of my life so far.
The movie ideally takes a rewatch to completely get the girl's perspective, and makes you feel every possible human feeling there is. It also has absolutely beautiful cinematography and mesmerizing, carefully-picked soundtracks. Although my favouritism towards the movie could've been influenced by my situation, but even while rewatching it after recovering, I still enjoyed it as much and felt that it had a lot more to offer that I missed during my first watch.

Disney/DreamWorks Movies

1. Brave
2. Zootopia
3. Soul
4. Penguins of Madagascar
5. Inside Out (Thanks to Radhika for Recommending)

Brave is and always will be my personal favourite. The initial monologue by Merida about fate gives me chills every time I listen to it and takes me right back to my childhood, when I watched it the first time. I remember really enjoying the movie and don't recall crying even a bit. But when I watched it in college after growing up, oddly enough, I ended up shedding tears at the climax. This movie has seen me mature emotionally with time.

Soul is another movie that I adore a lot. In fact, if I had to recommend one Disney movie that everyone should watch, it would be Soul, as it teaches us about what real happiness truly is, and if it can ever be achieved.

Series

1. Bojack Horseman
2. Dark
3. Sherlock
4. Gravity Falls
5. Blackadder
6. 13 Reasons Why (Season 1 only)

Bojack Horseman is the most psychologically clever series that has ever been made. It deals with topics that aren't usually talked about, and if such themes were to be portrayed by real human actors, the series would either be censored or banned. But through the animated characters and funny dialogue, you're constantly reminded that after all, it's a talking horse - how seriously are you actually supoosed to take him?
